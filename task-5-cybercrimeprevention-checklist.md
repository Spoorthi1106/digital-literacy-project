Phishing Prevention Checklist:
Check the source of your emails and messages carefully
Always scrutinize the sender's email address. Fake emails resemble original emails but have subtle differences.
Don’t click on dubious links
Steer clear from any unknown and untrusted links that may prompt urgent response.
Never share your personal and financial details
Do not divulge sensitive information like your passwords, OTPs, bank account numbers and PINs to any third party over the internet.
Cross-check the URL of your website carefully
Before providing any information, make sure your website is secure by looking for "https://" and double checking the spelling of the URL.
Use strong, unique and different passwords
Employ a combination of numbers, alphabets and special characters to form strong passwords for different accounts.
Enable two-factor authentication (2FA)
This will add an additional layer of security to your accounts thereby preventing intrusion of your personal data.
Ensure your devices and apps are updated regularly
Make sure your devices and apps are updated frequently to plug any security loopholes and protect your system from viruses and malicious cyber attacks.
Report cybercrime immediately
In case of any such incident of fraud, report it on the official website i.e. Cybercrime.gov.in or call up the help desk number i.e. 1930 for immediate action.
